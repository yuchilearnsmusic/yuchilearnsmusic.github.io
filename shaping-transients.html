Part One

Definitions

What is Compression?

What is Compression?

Volume Fader

It's just a really fast volume knob/fader that can only turn the volume down

What are transients?

To keep things simple, we're going to divide our sounds into two parts...

Transient

A transient is a spike in volume that occurs at the start of a sound

Body/Tail

The rest of the sound (the tone and decay)!

With compression, we can affect the volume of these parts of the sound individually!

Part Two

Identifying the Problem

Identifying Problems

So what is too loud or too soft?

But how do we make the transients or tails of sounds louder...

if compression can only decrease volume?

To make something louder, we have to make everything else quieter

Example: Making the Tail Louder

After making the transients quieter, the volume difference between the transients and the body/tail isn't as big

We can then increase the overall volume with makeup gain so that the compressed peak volume = uncompressed peak volume

Now the tail/body is louder than before!

How Much Volume?

Ratio Settings

Example

If the peak is 8dB above the threshold, and we have a ratio of 4:1, the peak will now only be 2dB above the threshold (1/4 of 8dB = 2dB)

Therefore, as the ratio gets higher, the signal above the threshold gets quieter and quieter

What Should My Ratio Be?

What Should My Ratio Be?

Setting Ratio

Start at 2:1. Keep increasing the ratio until the loud parts are as quiet as you want them to be!

Release Time

What Should My Release Time Be?
